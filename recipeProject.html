Gulab Jamun

Ingrediants

  • Milk Powder
  • Maida
  • Ghee
  • Milk
  • Oil

Recipe

  1. Gather Your Ingredients

  2. Sift the Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, sift together the milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Sifting is crucial as it aerates the mixture and removes any lumps, leading to a lighter jamun.

  3. Incorporate the Ghee: Add the melted ghee to the dry mixture. Using your fingertips, gently rub the ghee into the flour mixture until it resembles breadcrumbs. This process, called ‘moyan’, helps create a tender, non-chewy texture.

  4. Form the Dough: Now, add the warm milk, one tablespoon at a time. Do NOT knead this dough like bread. Use a light hand to bring the ingredients together until a very soft, slightly sticky dough forms. It’s better for the dough to be a little too soft than too hard. A hard dough guarantees hard gulab jamun. Let it rest for just 5-7 minutes.

  5. Shape the Balls:Grease your palms with a little ghee. Take small portions of the dough and roll them gently between your palms to form smooth, completely crack-free balls. Any cracks on the surface will expand and break open during frying. Be patient here; it’s worth

    the effort.

Milk Cake

Ingrediants

  • Milk
  • Sugar
  • Lemon Juice
  • Ghee

Recipe

  1. Gather Your Ingredients

  2. Reducing Milk: Boil full fat milk in a large pot. Cook milk on medium high heat till it comes to a boil. Keep scraping the sides of pot and cook the milk till reduced to ⅓rd of its original volume.

  3. Thickening Milk: Once the milk is reduced to ⅓rd of the original volume. Add in lemon juice little at a time, the milk mix will start to curdle a little. Keep cooking on medium high heat till thickens.

  4. Adding Sugar & Ghee: Once the milk cake mixture starts to get thick. Add little sugar at a time and keep cooking. Keep mixing and cooking and add little sugar at a time. Once you finish adding sugar, you can add ghee and cook till it comes together like a halwa.

  5. Moulding & Serving:Transfer the milk cake mix to a greased mould. Spread evenly so the top is smooth. Wrap the mould with foil and then wrap it with some cloth so it cools very slowly which results in the different shades of milk cake once you cut it. Once it is cooled for 4 to 5 hours, you can slice and serve.
